I am going to be looking at the perception of performative activism for college students in the downtown Portland area, specifically focusing on Portland State University students. During the protests of the murder of George Floyd, there was an influx of people publically supporting the cause but only for attention and, in some cases, to avoid ridicule for not speaking up. This happens on a personal level and at the corporate level, with brands posting similar smokescreen calls for justice and equality. It is significant to understand how these impact true activism, and where the line is.
